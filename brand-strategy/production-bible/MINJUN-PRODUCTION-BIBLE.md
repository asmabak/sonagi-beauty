## 1. CHARACTER IDENTITY BLOCK

Paste this into every prompt.

```
CHARACTER — MINJUN:
Young Korean male, early-to-mid 20s. Strikingly handsome with 
androgynous beauty — the look of a top K-pop idol or Korean actor. 
Perfectly symmetrical face. Sharp defined square jawline with clean 
angles. High cheekbones that catch warm light naturally. Large, 
expressive dark brown almond-shaped eyes with double eyelids — his 
eyes are his most expressive feature, always alive and reacting. 
Naturally full straight dark eyebrows, groomed but natural. Straight 
nose bridge with refined tip. Full, slightly pouty lips. Flawless 
porcelain skin with a natural luminous glow — smooth, even tone, no 
blemishes. Clean-shaven. Small silver stud earring in each ear.

BACKGROUND: Korean-American raised in the US, now living in Paris. 
He speaks English naturally — confident, warm, Gen-Z casual.

HAIR: Dark brown (almost black), tousled and textured, styled up 
and swept to the right with natural volume. Effortlessly messy — 
like he just ran his fingers through it. Medium length on top, 
shorter on sides, slightly wavy.

BUILD: Lean, slim. Visible collarbones. Slender neck.

WARDROBE: Simple fitted black crew-neck t-shirt. Clean, minimal.

ACCESSORIES: Small silver stud earring in each ear. A thin silver 
chain necklace with a small pendant resting on his collarbones. 
These small details make him feel like a real person, not a 
mannequin.

LANGUAGE: English. Natural American accent. All dialogue and 
captions in English. (French was tested and dropped — the AI 
voice sounded dull and unnatural.)
```

## 2. CAMERA & RECORDING STYLE

This is what makes it look like a real influencer video, not an ad.
Each clip uses ONE of the angles below. **Use a DIFFERENT angle for 
each clip** within a video — this is what makes multi-clip videos 
feel dynamic instead of repetitive. The camera is always STATIC 
within a single clip (phone propped somewhere, never moving).

### ANGLE A — Counter Low (default talking-to-camera angle)
```
CAMERA ANGLE A — COUNTER LOW:
Smartphone propped on the bathroom counter below face height, angled 
slightly upward at Minjun's face. He is looking slightly down into 
the camera — the natural angle of someone who placed their phone on 
a counter and hit record. Phone is approximately 40-50cm from his 
face. His face fills 70-80% of the frame. Very close, intimate, 
like a FaceTime call. He is slightly off-center, shifted to the 
left. Upper shoulders barely visible. Bathroom environment visible 
around him — tiles, mirror edge, counter items in the periphery.
The camera is completely STATIC for the entire clip.
```

### ANGLE B — Shelf High (looking up at camera)
```
CAMERA ANGLE B — SHELF HIGH:
Smartphone propped on a bathroom shelf or cabinet ABOVE face height, 
angled slightly downward at Minjun. He is looking slightly up into 
the camera — the angle of a phone placed on a high shelf. This 
creates a slightly more vulnerable, open feeling — his face is 
tilted up, jawline softened, eyes looking up. Phone is approximately 
50-60cm from his face. His face fills 60-70% of the frame. Head, 
shoulders and upper chest visible. More of the counter and bathroom 
below him is visible. He is slightly off-center. The camera is 
completely STATIC for the entire clip.
```

### ANGLE C — Mirror Side (three-quarter angle)
```
CAMERA ANGLE C — MIRROR SIDE:
Smartphone propped on the counter to Minjun's left side, angled to 
capture him at a three-quarter view — not straight-on but not full 
profile. He turns his head to look at the camera, creating a natural 
slight angle on his face. The round bathroom mirror is visible 
behind his opposite shoulder. This angle shows more of the bathroom 
environment and feels like the viewer caught him mid-routine. Phone 
is approximately 50cm away. His face fills 60-70% of the frame. 
The camera is completely STATIC for the entire clip.
```

### ANGLE D — Close Selfie (handheld, for outdoor/travel clips)
```
CAMERA ANGLE D — CLOSE SELFIE:
Minjun is holding the phone himself at arm's length, slightly above 
eye level, angled down at his face. Slight natural hand movement — 
not shaky but not perfectly stable. Very close framing — his face 
fills 80-90% of the frame. Background is real-world (street, cafe, 
store) with natural bokeh. This is the "walking and talking" or 
"just had to share this" angle. Only for outdoor/travel content.
```

### Universal camera rules (apply to ALL angles):
```
UNIVERSAL CAMERA RULES:
- The camera is ALWAYS STATIC within a single clip (except Angle D 
  which has slight natural hand movement)
- ALL movement comes from Minjun — he leans in, leans back, tilts 
  his head, turns to grab a product
- Minjun is NEVER perfectly centered in the frame — always slightly 
  off-center
- The environment is ALWAYS visible around him — never a blank void
- Each clip in a video uses a DIFFERENT angle for visual variety
- Within one clip, the angle NEVER changes

DO NOT USE: Tracking shots, dolly movements, smooth push-ins, macro 
close-ups, drone angles, cinematic shallow depth of field with heavy 
bokeh, perfectly centered portrait framing, or any camera movement 
within a clip.
```

## 3. INTERACTION & PERFORMANCE STYLE

Minjun's on-camera energy is inspired by **Edward Zo** — playful, 
witty, charismatic, intimate. The camera is a person, not a lens.

Reference reels studied (saved in minjun-reference/):
- edwardzo-reel1.jpg: Street filming, hand on face, side-glance, 
  slightly angled head, provocative text hook at top
- edwardzo-reel2.jpg: Indoor, phone at face height, mid-sentence 
  talking directly at lens, challenging expression, tight framing
- edwardzo-reel3.jpg: Sitting, relaxed amused energy, "let me show 
  you" vibe, natural room visible behind, accessories (necklace, rings)

```
INTERACTION & PERFORMANCE STYLE:

Minjun looks DIRECTLY into the camera lens as if it is his best 
friend sitting on the counter in front of him. He is mid-conversation 
with this person. He is relaxed, warm, slightly playful — never 
stiff, never performing, never posing.

HIS EYES (the most important element):
- Eyes are locked on the lens 80% of the time — warm, direct, 
  engaged, like a FaceTime call with someone he likes
- He breaks eye contact only to glance down at a product in his 
  hands, then looks back up at the camera with a micro-reaction: 
  a raised eyebrow, a small smirk, a knowing look
- Playful eye movements: a quick side-glance then back to camera 
  (the "you know what I mean" look), eyes that widen just before 
  he says something surprising, eyes that narrow slightly when 
  sharing a secret
- His eyes are NEVER blank or neutral — they are always expressing 
  something: curiosity, amusement, warmth, mischief, sincerity
- When he applies product, his eyes stay half-open and he glances 
  at the camera mid-application with a small "this feels good" 
  smile — he shares the moment with the viewer

HIS FACE (constantly in motion):
- His expression shifts every 1-2 seconds — he never holds a 
  single expression for long
- Talking: natural mouth movement, slightly asymmetric, sometimes 
  biting his lower lip briefly when thinking
- Smirking: one corner of mouth lifts, confident but warm — his 
  go-to expression between sentences
- Surprised: eyebrows shoot up, mouth opens slightly, then 
  immediately transitions to another expression
- Amused: quick exhale through nose, slight head shake, half-smile 
  — self-aware, never takes himself too seriously
- Sincere: softer eyes, smaller smile, slight head tilt — for 
  emotional or vulnerable moments

HIS BODY:
- Head tilts frequently — left, right, small nods while talking
- Shoulders relaxed, slightly asymmetric, loose
- One hand gestures casually while talking — not theatrical, just 
  natural emphasis
- He leans toward the camera when making a point or sharing a 
  secret, leans back when transitioning or reaching for a product
- When picking up a product, he glances at it casually, brings it 
  into frame without ceremony, shows it briefly, then puts it down

WHAT HE NEVER DOES:
- Never stares at camera with a fixed frozen smile
- Never holds a pose for more than 1 second
- Never "presents" a product with both hands held up like a TV ad
- Never tilts his face upward with eyes closed (beauty ad pose)
- Never looks away from camera for more than 2 seconds continuously
- Never stands stiff and perfectly centered
- Never makes exaggerated theatrical expressions — everything is 
  subtle, controlled, confident
```

## 4. PRODUCT & TEXTURE RENDERING

The product must look REAL on skin. Seedance v1 produced completely 
dry skin during application — this section prevents that.

```
PRODUCT & TEXTURE RULES:

DISPENSING (when he pours or pumps product):
- The liquid is CLEARLY VISIBLE: a stream or drops flowing from the 
  bottle into his cupped palm
- The liquid pools in his palm — we see it sitting there, catching 
  the bathroom light, slightly reflective and wet-looking
- He uses products GENEROUSLY — more than expected. His palm has a 
  visible pool of product. The "satisfying excess" look that skincare 
  TikTok loves
- He glances down at the product in his hand, then looks up at 
  camera with a small nod or raised eyebrow before applying

APPLICATION (when he puts product on his face):
- His fingers and palms are VISIBLY WET AND GLISTENING with product 
  before they touch his face
- He applies with his FINGERTIPS — gentle rhythmic tapping and 
  pressing on his cheeks, forehead, nose. Not flat palms covering 
  his entire face
- His face is ALWAYS VISIBLE during application — we can see his 
  eyes, his expression, his reaction. His hands never fully cover 
  or obscure his face
- As product touches his skin, the skin becomes VISIBLY WET AND 
  DEWY — light reflects off the hydrated surface, there is a clear 
  visible difference between dry skin and product-applied skin
- He enjoys the ritual — slight satisfied smile, relaxed jaw, 
  half-closed eyes that keep glancing at the camera to share the 
  pleasure with the viewer

AFTER APPLICATION:
- His skin has a visible wet luminous sheen — light bouncing off 
  cheeks, forehead, nose
- The "glass skin" effect: his face looks dewy, hydrated, glowing
- This is NOT subtle — the dewiness should be clearly visible even 
  on a phone screen

WHAT TO AVOID:
- Dry hands touching a dry face with no visible product
- Miming application without any liquid visible
- Eyes fully closed during entire application
- Hands covering his whole face so viewer can't see him
- Product appearing already absorbed with no visible wet stage
- Face looking exactly the same before and after application
```

## 5. BATHROOM SETTING

Paste into every bathroom-scene prompt.

```
SETTING — MINJUN'S BATHROOM:

A modern but lived-in bathroom. This is a real person's space, not 
a photography studio.

WALLS: Warm beige-grey large-format tiles covering the walls. Clean 
but not sterile. Subtle grout lines visible. The color is WARM — 
cream, beige, soft grey — never cold white or flat dark grey.

COUNTER: White stone or light marble countertop visible to Minjun's 
right side (viewer's left). On the counter: 4-5 Korean skincare 
bottles and jars of varying sizes, casually clustered together — 
not arranged in a perfect line, grouped naturally as a real person 
would leave them after their routine. One bottle is on its side.

MIRROR: A round mirror with a thin brass or gold frame mounted on 
the wall behind and to one side of Minjun. The mirror has warm LED 
backlighting that creates a soft ambient glow on the wall. The 
mirror is slightly out of focus but recognizable.

LIGHTING: The main light source is a warm-toned vanity light or 
wall sconce on the left side, casting soft directional light across 
Minjun's face from the left. This creates gentle shadows on the 
right side of his face that naturally sculpt his jawline and 
cheekbones. Color temperature is WARM — golden, inviting, like 
late afternoon indoor light. There may be a secondary soft light 
from above (bathroom ceiling light) but it is not harsh.

SMALL DETAILS: A folded white hand towel on the counter. A small 
green plant (pothos) in a simple white pot near the mirror. These 
details make the space feel real and personal.

ATMOSPHERE: Warm, intimate, cozy — the feeling of being invited 
into someone's private evening skincare ritual. The bathroom feels 
comfortable, not clinical.

WHAT THIS SETTING IS NOT:
- Not a flat grey or white photography backdrop
- Not a dark moody space
- Not a professional studio with controlled lighting rigs
- Not an empty room with nothing in it
- Not harshly lit with cool white fluorescent light
```

## 6. TEXT HOOK OVERLAYS (post-production)

Every video needs a bold text hook at the top of the frame in the 
first 1-2 seconds. This is added in the editor, not by Seedance.

```
TEXT HOOK RULES:

PLACEMENT: Top 15-20% of the frame, above Minjun's head. Large 
bold text, usually 2-3 lines.

STYLE: Bold sans-serif (Montserrat Black or similar). White text 
with a subtle dark shadow or outline for readability. Sometimes 
a key word is highlighted in yellow or red.

PURPOSE: Provocative or curiosity-driven — makes the viewer stop 
scrolling. This is the first thing they read before Minjun starts 
talking.

EXAMPLES:
- "my skin was DESTROYED" (Script 05)
- "one of the WORST beauty trends from Korea" (Edward Zo style)
- "a girl stopped me to ask THIS" (Script 04)
- "don't even try." (challenging, Edward Zo style)

Can also include a small PHOTO overlay (celebrity, product, meme) 
next to or below the text — the TikTok "reference image" format 
seen in the inspiration screenshot (Jungkook example).
```

## 7. BEFORE/AFTER FORMAT

How to handle transformation comparisons in Minjun's videos. This 
is based on how real skincare TikTok creators do it — NOT how ads 
do it.

```
BEFORE/AFTER RULES:

NEVER generate a split-screen side-by-side comparison within the 
Seedance video. This looks like a clinical ad, not a creator's 
content.

Instead, before/after is handled in TWO ways:

METHOD 1 — OVERLAY PHOTOS (added in post-production):
Two small rectangular photos are placed at the TOP of the video 
frame during editing (in CapCut or Opus Clip). One labeled "Before" 
and one labeled "After." These float above Minjun's head while he 
continues talking to camera below them. The photos are added in the 
video editor, NOT generated by Seedance.

METHOD 2 — NATURAL IN-VIDEO TRANSITION:
Clip A shows Minjun with visible skin issues (redness, dryness — 
described in the prompt). Clip C shows him after application with 
visibly dewy, calm, glowing skin. The before/after happens 
NATURALLY across the clips through the change in his skin's 
appearance — not through a split screen or graphic effect.

For Seedance prompts: simply describe the state of his skin in 
each clip. Clip A = "skin shows visible redness and slight dryness 
on cheeks." Clip C = "skin is visibly dewy, hydrated, calm, and 
glowing after product application."
```

## 8. CLIP STRUCTURE — Building 45s videos from 3 x 15s clips

Higgsfield max duration: **15 seconds per generation.**

### Template:

| Clip | Duration | Purpose | Recommended Angle |
|------|----------|---------|-------------------|
| **A — Hook & Setup** | 15s | Grab attention, establish topic/problem | **Angle B (Shelf High)** — slightly vulnerable, open, draws viewer in |
| **B — Product & Application** | 15s | Product reveal, pouring, application | **Angle C (Mirror Side)** — shows hands/product better at three-quarter view, bathroom context visible |
| **C — Result & Close** | 15s | Transformation, reaction, sign-off | **Angle A (Counter Low)** — classic intimate direct-to-camera for the emotional close |

The angle changes between clips create **visual dynamism** — each 
cut feels like a natural shift (he moved his phone) rather than a 
repetitive static shot. This is what makes multi-clip videos feel 
professional and engaging rather than three copies of the same frame.

### Rules:

1. **One continuous take per clip** from a static phone camera. No 
   angle changes within a single clip.

2. **Each clip uses a DIFFERENT camera angle** from Section 2. This 
   is mandatory — never use the same angle for two consecutive clips.

3. **Slight overlap between clips** — end of A connects naturally to 
   start of B (e.g., A ends with him reaching for a product, B starts 
   with him already holding it).

4. **French dialogue written to fit 15 seconds** — approximately 30-40 
   words of spoken French per clip.

5. **Voice generated by Higgsfield** — Higgsfield produces better voice 
   than ElevenLabs for this character (confirmed by testing).

6. **Captions added in post-production** — not in the Seedance prompt. 
   A separate caption spec is written per script for the video editor.

7. **Text hook overlay added in post-production** — bold text at top 
   of frame for Clip A. See Section 6.

8. **Before/after overlay photos added in post-production** — not 
   generated by Seedance. See Section 7.

9. **Transitions between clips** handled in editor — simple cuts or 
   very short cross-dissolves.

### Prompt template for each clip:

```
[CHARACTER BLOCK — Section 1]

[CAMERA ANGLE — pick one from Section 2, different per clip]

[INTERACTION BLOCK — Section 3]

[PRODUCT BLOCK — Section 4, only for Clips B and C]

[SETTING BLOCK — Section 5]

---

SCENE: [What happens in this specific 15-second clip]

MINJUN'S SKIN STATE: [Describe current skin appearance]

MINJUN'S ENERGY: [vulnerable / excited / warm / conspiratorial / 
satisfied / sincere]

FRENCH DIALOGUE (Minjun speaks directly to camera):
"[30-40 words of French]"

VOICE DIRECTION: [Specific notes — soft and honest / excited and 
fast / warm and calm]
```

## 9. CAPTION STYLE (post-production reference)

Captions are added in the video editor AFTER stitching clips.

### Format:
- One word appears at a time, synced to speech rhythm
- Each word varies in size, color, weight, or position
- Key words POP large and bold
- Filler words stay small and white

### Color palette:

| Word type | Color | Size | Weight |
|-----------|-------|------|--------|
| Normal filler (the, a, is, and) | White #FFFFFF | Small 18-22pt | Regular |
| Product names | Coral Pink #FF6B6B | Med-Large 28-34pt | Bold |
| Action words (try, use, apply) | Cyan #00E5FF | Medium 24-28pt | Semi-bold |
| Emphasis (INSANE, CRAZY, NEVER) | Bright Yellow #FFD700 | XL 36-44pt | Extra-bold |
| Negative words (don't, stop) | Red #FF3333 | Large 30-36pt | Bold |
| Korean terms (chok-chok) | Lavender #B388FF | Med-Large 28-32pt | Italic bold |
| Numbers and stats | Green #00FF88 | Large 30-34pt | Bold |

### Font: Montserrat Bold (primary), Nunito (soft words)
### Position: Lower-center of frame (platform safe zone)
### Animation: Pop-in with bounce (default), slam for impact words

## 10. ANTI-PATTERNS — Mistakes from v1 to never repeat

| v1 Problem | Bible Rule |
|---|---|
| Flat grey studio background | Full bathroom setting with tiles, counter, mirror, objects |
| Perfectly centered portrait framing | Off-center, phone-on-counter angle, face fills 70-80% |
| Camera pushes in / pulls back / pans | Camera is STATIC. Minjun moves, camera doesn't |
| Model "presenting" product like a commercial | Casual hold, glances at product, no ceremony |
| Dry skin during product application | Visible liquid in palm, wet glistening hands, dewy skin |
| Eyes closed, face tilted up during application | Eyes half-open, glancing at camera, face visible |
| Hands covering entire face | Fingertip tapping on cheeks, face always visible |
| Same angle for every clip in a video | Each clip uses a different angle for dynamism |
| Hard jump-cuts between different angles | Single angle per clip, smooth natural movement |
| Static frozen facial expression | Constantly shifting — smirk, surprise, warmth, amusement |
| Split-screen before/after comparison | Overlay photos in post, or natural skin change across clips |
| Cinematic polished ad look | Raw phone-on-counter authenticity |
| Full body or wide shots | Head and shoulders only, tight intimate framing |
| Macro extreme close-ups of skin | No macro — just the natural phone-camera distance |
