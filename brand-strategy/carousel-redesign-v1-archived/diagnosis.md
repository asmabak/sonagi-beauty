## 2. Five Recurring Problems Across Rejected Set

1. **Headline fragmentation into mismatched type sizes.** Pattern of splitting a single sentence into 3 lines where line 2 or line 3 becomes a huge peach word. Reader has to reassemble the sentence. Worst cases: may11, may12, may17, may21. Published winners (may03 "Frotter = rides. Tapoter = glass skin.", may05 "Un seul nettoyage ne suffit pas.") keep the headline as ONE visual block.

2. **French quality / typos / franglais.** "peau prête pas trempée" (may04), "a TOI" missing accent (may11), "demaquillante" missing é (may15 s2), "divise par 5" missing é (may21), "L'HA" undefined abbreviation (may11). Per `feedback_french_accents.md` — ALL French content must have proper accents. Zero tolerance.

3. **Jargon dropped as body copy.** "Polydeoxyribonucléotide" (may12 s2), "HA" without expansion (may11), "layering coréen" without context (may17). Reader can't decode → can't convert.

4. **Real-estate waste on interior slides.** Bottom 40–50% empty on may12 s2, may15 s2-s3, may11 s2. Headline + 3 body lines + tiny CTA bar = 500px used, 500px white. QC "disastrous usage of real estate" refers to this, not slide-1.

5. **Competitor brand logos visible in photography.** may11 and may15 use the same stock photo of 6-7 serums where The Ordinary, iUnik, Centella/SKIN1004, Cos De BAHA, Mizon are all readable. Sonagi is pre-launch, owns no inventory, has no resale rights. This is the legal concern (see section 4 below).

6. **Kicker label missing, wrong color, or redundant.** may12 and may04 have no topic kicker — reader doesn't know what the carousel is about. may15 kicker "ROUTINE COMPLÈTE" is muted peach → invisible. may19 kicker "5 MINUTES" just repeats the headline. Published winners (may03 "ROUTINE K-BEAUTY", may05 "K-BEAUTY ESSENTIEL") use high-contrast peach kickers that frame the topic.

7. **Sub-hook either accusatory or making unverifiable claims.** "Et tu ne t'en rends même pas compte" (may04), "Mêmes résultats. Budget divisé par 5" (may21). Per `feedback_no_exaggerated_claims.md` — Sonagi doesn't own products and can't claim clinical equivalence.

## 3. What Published Carousels Do Right

Comparing may01, may02, may03, may05:

- **Headline is ONE coherent visual block, not a word puzzle.** may03: "Frotter = rides. Tapoter = glass skin." is one sentence in white serif. may05: "Un seul nettoyage ne suffit pas." is one sentence. The reader processes it in one pass.
- **Peach is used as a single accent, not as a competing type color.** may01 uses peach only on the numeral "7". may05 uses peach only on the kicker "K-BEAUTY ESSENTIEL". Contrast vs rejected carousels where peach takes half the headline weight.
- **Kicker label defines the topic before the headline.** "ROUTINE K-BEAUTY", "K-BEAUTY ESSENTIEL", "LE PROBLÈME", "COMPARATIF". Frames what the reader is about to consume.
- **Sub-hook is a complete sentence tying headline to payoff.** may05: "Ton nettoyant habituel ne retire que 50% de ce qu'il y a sur ta peau" — specific, factual, pays off the headline.
- **Product photography when used is either generic textures (mint bottles may20 — ambiguous brand) or faces/hands on skin.** may02 IS the exception — it uses the same competitor serum shelf as may11 and may15. Published but risky. Should be treated as legacy and not repeated.

## 4. Real-Product Logos — Legal and Brand Analysis

**Legal view (France / EU):**
Displaying competitor product photography in your marketing materials is governed by French law L.713-3 and L.713-6 (Code de la propriété intellectuelle) and comparative advertising law L.121-8 (Code de la consommation). Purely decorative/editorial use (e.g. a magazine article showing a shelf of products) is generally tolerated. BUT when the use implies endorsement, resale, or comparison without strict conditions, you enter risky territory:

- **Implied resale / stocking:** If an Instagram post for a K-beauty brand shows The Ordinary and iUnik bottles with no disclaimer, the average viewer assumes Sonagi sells them. Sonagi does not. This creates consumer confusion (tromperie) risk and potential trademark dilution claims from the brand owners.
- **Comparative claims:** may21's "200€ vs 40€" + brand logos elsewhere in the set would trigger L.121-8 which requires claims be verifiable, not misleading, and comparing comparable products. Sonagi cannot meet those conditions pre-launch.
- **Affiliate/PR fair use:** None of these brands have issued Sonagi press samples or affiliate codes (project is pre-launch, no supplier relationships confirmed). No fair-use defence.

**Brand view:**
Showing The Ordinary next to the Sonagi logo positions Sonagi as The Ordinary's retailer. Sonagi's brand strategy (per `canva-specs.md` and brand-identity folder) is curation and storytelling — not reselling a commodity shelf. Showing a generic rainbow of bottles undermines the "I chose these for you" curator voice.

**Recommendation — enforce starting next batch:**

> **Rule: Any product shown in Sonagi carousels must be either (a) in Sonagi's confirmed resale catalog with a supplier agreement on file, OR (b) visually unbranded — labels removed, flipped, blurred, or photographed as abstract textures (drops, cream, oil, botanical ingredients). Competitor brand photography (The Ordinary, iUnik, COSRX, Mizon, Centella/SKIN1004, Cos De BAHA, Anua, Banila Co, etc.) is BANNED from all slides until resale agreements are signed.**

Safe substitutes: close-ups of skin/hands applying product, ingredient shots (centella leaves, snail mucin texture, rice grains), texture shots (serum drops, cream swirls, water beads), AI-generated abstract product mockups with no brand marks. See production-bible.md Minjun rules.

The may02 and may11/may15 shelf photo should be retired across the whole carousel system, not just fixed per-slide.
