**Status:** Pre-launch (April 2026)
**Audience:** French women 18-35 curious about K-beauty
**Brand voice:** Provocative Educator (more edge than Soko Glam, more intimate than Glow Recipe)
**Author:** Senior YouTube strategist briefing
**Last updated:** 2026-04-17

---

## EXECUTIVE VERDICT (READ THIS FIRST)

**Should Sonagi build YouTube pre-launch? Yes — but Shorts-only, not hybrid.**

YouTube is the single highest-effort, slowest-payoff platform in social. Long-form (8-15 min videos) demands a filming setup, scripting, editing time of 8-12 hours per video, a face on camera, and 6-12 months before the algorithm trusts a new channel. Sonagi pre-launch does not have that bandwidth, and Asma's energy is better spent on TikTok/Instagram where one viral post can move pre-launch waitlist signups in 48 hours.

**But not having a YouTube presence at all is a mistake.** Three reasons:
1. Search behavior — French women Google "routine coréenne débutante" and YouTube results dominate page one. If Sonagi has zero footprint, a competitor (Yepoda, Korean Skincare France, Skinorea) owns that intent.
2. Cross-pollination — Minjun videos already exist in 45-second format. Posting them as Shorts costs ~5 minutes per upload. Free reach.
3. Long-tail compounding — a Short posted today can still be pulling views in 18 months. TikTok content goes dead in 7 days.

**The play:** Open the channel this week. Post Shorts only (Minjun reposts + carousel slideshows + occasional Asma face-to-camera). Hold long-form until post-launch when there's product in stock to actually film.

### YouTube algorithm + monetization reality (2026)

- **Shorts and long-form are now separate algorithms** since the 2025-2026 split. Shorts no longer drag down or boost long-form.
- **Monetization thresholds:**
  - Standard YPP: 1,000 subs + 4,000 watch hours OR 10M Shorts views in 90 days
  - Lower fan-funding tier: 500 subs + 3M Shorts views in 90 days OR 3,000 watch hours
- **Shorts RPM:** $0.03-$0.10 per 1,000 views in beauty/lifestyle (much lower than finance at 10x). At 1M Shorts views/month, that's roughly $30-100. **Shorts will not pay Sonagi's bills. Brand/affiliate revenue will.**
- **March 2026 update:** RPM now weighted by engagement signals — 3-minute Shorts earn 2-3x the RPM of 15-second clips. Worth noting for future Minjun stitches.
- **Funnel data:** Shorts convert viewers to subs at ~0.8%, long-form at ~2.3%. Channels mixing both grow ~41% faster than single-format. But this requires capacity Sonagi doesn't have yet.

### What French K-beauty YouTube looks like in 2026

The French K-beauty YT space is thin. Search "routine coréenne" and you get:
- A few brand channels (Yepoda, Korean Skincare France) that read as ads
- One or two niche curators (Elora as Sweet as Honey)
- Dermatologists (Dermato Drey) who occasionally touch K-beauty
- Lots of one-off videos from generalist beauty YouTubers who tried K-products once

**Gap:** There is no French equivalent of Liah Yoo or James Welsh. No French personality has claimed "K-beauty curator with edge." Sonagi can own that lane — but it's a long-form lane, and long-form is post-launch territory.

### 2. Strategic decision: Shorts-only vs Hybrid (the honest answer)

**Recommendation: Shorts-only for the first 90 days post-launch.** Then re-evaluate.

**Why Shorts-only wins pre-launch:**
- Minjun videos already exist in 45-second format — zero new production cost
- French carousels can become slideshow Shorts (10-12 frames at 1.5s each + audio) — zero new production cost
- TikToks already produced can cross-post to YT Shorts with a description rewrite — 5 min/upload
- One-format focus means one set of analytics to read, not two
- Long-form requires 8-12 hours per video that Asma should be spending on TikTok/Instagram where conversion is faster

**When to flip the switch to hybrid:** post-launch, when (a) product is in stock and unboxings/routines can be filmed, (b) Asma can commit to one 8-12 min video per month, (c) channel has hit 1k subs from Shorts and there's a warm audience to serve long-form to.

**The trap to avoid:** Posting one bad long-form video at month 2 because someone said "you need long-form." A bad long-form (low retention, low CTR) signals to YouTube that the channel is low-quality and depresses Shorts distribution too. Wait until you can do it right.

### 3. Shorts strategy

**Frequency:** 4-5 Shorts per week (not 7 — overposting dilutes thumbnail learning). Mix sources:
- 2x Minjun reposts (English, captioned in French)
- 1-2x carousel slideshow Shorts (French)
- 1x TikTok cross-post (French, Asma face-to-camera if available)

**Length sweet spot:** 25-45 seconds. Avoid 8-15s clips — March 2026 RPM update penalizes them. Aim for 30s as default.

**Vertical format:** 1080x1920, 9:16. Safe zone: keep all text/faces in the central 60% — the bottom 240px is covered by UI on mobile.

**Hooks (different from TikTok!):**
- TikTok hooks reward chaos and sound design. YouTube Shorts hooks reward clarity and curiosity gap.
- First 2 seconds must answer: "what am I about to learn?"
- Top hook formats for K-beauty:
  - "POV: ta peau française découvre la double cleansing pour la première fois"
  - "Le seul produit coréen que je rachète depuis 3 ans"
  - "Pourquoi ta crème hydratante ne fait rien (et quoi mettre à la place)"
  - "Dermatologue coréen vs pharmacie française: qui gagne?"
  - "Ce que les Coréennes mettent AVANT le sérum (et que personne ne te dit)"

**The 3-second rule:** 50-60% of viewers who drop off do so in the first 3 seconds. Aim for VVSA (Viewed vs Swiped Away) above 70%. If a Short underperforms 60% intro retention, don't reuse that hook structure.

**Sounds:** YouTube Shorts has a much smaller trending-sound library than TikTok. **Use original audio** for Minjun (his voice is the asset) and **license-free music** for slideshow Shorts (Epidemic Sound or YouTube Audio Library). Do not reuse TikTok trending sounds — they often won't be available on YT.

**Captions:** Burn in French captions for every Short (even Minjun's English ones). YouTube auto-captions are decent but slow to render — burned-in is more reliable for the first frame hook. Use Sonagi brand fonts (Georgia for elegance moments, Arial for punchy callouts).

**End screen / CTA on Shorts:**
- Last 2 seconds: text overlay "Abonne-toi pour ta routine coréenne hebdo"
- First pinned comment: link to sonagibeauty.com waitlist
- Description: 2-line hook + 5 hashtags max + waitlist link

### 4. If long-form (post-launch month 4+): 5 formats that work for K-beauty curators

When the time comes, these are the highest-leverage formats. Pick ONE and own it for 3 months before adding a second.

**Format A — "I tried [trending Korean product] for 30 days" (review-vlog hybrid)**
- 8-12 min, day-by-day with skin shots
- High search volume ("beauty of joseon serum review français")
- Asma face-to-camera + product shots
- Title pattern: "J'ai testé [PRODUIT] pendant 30 jours · résultat honnête"

**Format B — "Korean skincare routine of a French girl with [skin type]" (POV routine)**
- 6-10 min, real-time-ish routine with product reasoning
- High intent — people search exact routines
- Title pattern: "Ma routine coréenne · peau mixte sensible · printemps 2026"

**Format C — Ingredient deep-dive (Hyram-lite)**
- 8-15 min, single ingredient, what it does, what to look for, Sonagi's pick
- Builds authority → trust → store conversion
- Title pattern: "Niacinamide expliquée: pourquoi 90% des Françaises s'en servent mal"

**Format D — Brand-by-brand comparison**
- 10-15 min, "Beauty of Joseon vs COSRX vs [third brand]" head-to-head
- Massive search volume in English, growing in French
- Title pattern: "Beauty of Joseon vs COSRX: j'ai testé les 6 sérums les plus vendus"

**Format E — "Korean derm reacts to French pharmacy skincare" (collab/character format)**
- This is where Minjun could become a long-form character — scripted reactions to French pharmacy products
- Higher production cost (Seedance 3-4 stitched 15s clips + voiceover + b-roll)
- But novelty is huge — nobody is doing this in French K-beauty YT

**Recommended first long-form when launch happens:** Format B. Lowest production cost, highest search intent, fastest to film once product is in stock.

### 5. Title + thumbnail framework

**Title rules (CTR-optimized):**
- Keep under 60 characters (mobile cutoff)
- Front-load the keyword that matches search intent
- Include a number, a contrast, or a curiosity gap
- French accents required (é è ê à ç ô û î) — never skip them, both for SEO and credibility

**Title patterns that work for K-beauty:**
- Number + benefit: "5 produits coréens qui ont sauvé ma peau d'hiver"
- Contrast: "Pharmacie française vs K-beauty: le test honnête"
- Curiosity gap: "Pourquoi les Coréennes ne se lavent pas le visage le matin"
- Personal stake: "J'ai arrêté ma routine française pendant 60 jours · voici ce qui s'est passé"
- Question: "Le snail mucin vaut-il vraiment le buzz?"

**Thumbnail rules (1280x720):**
- Bold colors that pop on YouTube's white/gray UI: warm peach (#F4C7A8 from Sonagi spec), navy (#1F2A44), occasional red accent for contrast
- Face with strong emotion (Asma surprised, intrigued, slightly skeptical) — emotional faces increase CTR up to 30%
- Text under 5 words, "postage stamp" readable on mobile
- High contrast between text and background
- Product hero shot OR face hero shot, never both fighting for attention
- DO NOT use fake product packaging — only real product (when in stock) or texture/ritual shots
- Test 2-3 thumbnails per long-form video using YouTube's "Test & Compare" once eligible

**Beware the "thumbnail-content alignment paradox":** YouTube's algorithm now penalizes high-CTR thumbnails that don't deliver retention. No clickbait. The thumbnail must accurately preview the actual video.

- **VidIQ free plan** (recommended for sub-5K subs) — AI Coach, daily video ideas, basic keyword research
- TubeBuddy Pro ($2.25/mo) — only worth it after 1k subs for A/B testing

### 7. Cross-platform pipeline (the actual recipe)

Sonagi already produces content for TikTok, Instagram, and Minjun. YouTube should be the lowest-effort downstream platform.

**Recipe 1: Minjun video → YT Short**
1. Take the rendered 45-second Minjun stitch (3x15s Seedance clips)
2. Re-export as 1080x1920 if not already
3. Add burned-in French captions (Asma writes a 30-word translation of Minjun's English voiceover)
4. Add Sonagi watermark bottom-right (small, 60% opacity)
5. Upload to YT Shorts with title pattern: "[Curiosity hook in French]" + description with waitlist link
6. Total time: 8-12 minutes per video

**Recipe 2: Carousel → YT slideshow Short**
1. Take the 7-slide French carousel (1080x1080)
2. Resize each slide to 1080x1920 (add cream-color top/bottom bars to fill)
3. Drop into CapCut or Canva video → 1.8 seconds per slide → 12-13 sec video
4. Add a soft instrumental from YT Audio Library
5. Add a CTA card at the end (3 sec): "Routine complète sur sonagibeauty.com"
6. Upload as Short with first carousel hook as title
7. Total time: 15 minutes per carousel

**Recipe 3: TikTok → YT Short**
1. Download TikTok video without watermark (CapCut export or SnapTik)
2. Replace TikTok-specific sound if it's a trending sound (use original audio or licensed library track)
3. Rewrite description for YT (no @-mentions of TikTok creators, add waitlist link, change hashtags from #fyp to #routinecoreenne)
4. Upload
5. Total time: 5 minutes

**Recipe 4: Long-form (post-launch) → 3-5 Shorts**
- Every 8-12 min long-form should generate 3-5 Shorts (best moments, hooks, conclusions)
- Use Opus Clip or CapCut auto-clip for first pass, edit by hand
- This doubles the content output per filming day

### 8. Monetization timeline

**Realistic timeline for Sonagi (Shorts-only for 90 days, then hybrid):**
- Month 1-3: 0 revenue. Goal is subs and views, not money.
- Month 4-6: If hitting ~50K Shorts views/month, still likely under threshold. Start tracking.
- Month 6-9: Monetization eligible if either threshold hit (1k subs + 4k watch hrs OR 1k subs + 10M Shorts views in 90 days). 10M Shorts views is HARD — don't bank on it.
- Month 9-12: First ad payouts in beauty niche: realistic $30-150/month at 100K-500K monthly views. **This is rounding error.**

**The real money:** Affiliate (Sonagi's own store), brand sponsorships (once at 10K+ subs and an audience that converts), and shopping shelf integration (when YT Shopping rolls out fully in France, expected 2026-2027).

**Set expectations:** YouTube ad revenue from Shorts will not pay for the production time. Treat YouTube as a top-of-funnel SEO and trust-building channel. Conversion happens on sonagibeauty.com.

### 9. Community tab

**Unlocks at 500 subs.** Until then, ignore.

**Once unlocked, posting cadence:** 1-3 posts per week (not more). Channels posting 3+/week see 18-25% higher subscriber retention.

**Best post types for K-beauty:**
- Polls (3-4x more engagement than text): "Quel produit coréen veux-tu que je teste cette semaine?" with 4 options
- Behind-the-scenes images: Sonagi packaging in progress, samples arriving from Korea, Asma's actual bathroom shelf
- Mini-blog posts: 200 words on a single ingredient, posted between videos
- Teaser clips: 5-10 sec preview of next video
- Question stickers: "Quelle est ta plus grosse galère skincare en ce moment?"

**Timing:** Check YT analytics for when audience is online; aim for 50-70% of audience online when posting.

**The pinned community post strategy:** Always pin a poll or a CTA to the latest waitlist signup count. Creates social proof.

### 10. Engagement tactics

**First-hour rule:** Reply to every comment in the first hour after posting. The algorithm reads comment velocity as a quality signal. Set a calendar block: post at 18h French time → reply 18h-19h.

**Pinned comment strategy:** Every video gets a pinned comment from @sonagibeauty with:
- A direct link to the most-relevant store page or waitlist
- A question to drive replies ("Quel sera le prochain produit coréen que vous voulez que je teste?")

**Heart hearts:** Heart 5-10 of the best comments per video. Costs 30 seconds, signals to the commenter that they're seen, makes them come back.

**Reply with a Short:** YouTube lets you reply to a comment with a video Short. Powerful for "how do I use X?" questions — answer with a 20-second Short, tag the original commenter. Builds parasocial loyalty.

**Shorts comments:** Reply with text only on Shorts (video replies don't apply). Ask follow-up questions to keep the comment thread alive — long threads = algorithmic boost.

### 14. What NOT to do

- Do NOT post a Short more than 5x/week. Diminishing returns + dilutes thumbnail learning.
- Do NOT reuse TikTok trending sounds. Half won't be available on YT, and the rest don't carry the same algorithmic weight.
- Do NOT make a channel trailer in month 1. Wait for content body.
- Do NOT pay for subscribers, sub-for-sub, or any growth hack. YT's spam detection will tank distribution.
- Do NOT post long-form before launch. One bad long-form depresses the whole channel's distribution.
- Do NOT use fake product packaging in Minjun videos — credibility-killer per project rules.
- Do NOT skip French accents. é è ê à ç ô û î required everywhere.
- Do NOT make miracle claims ("ce sérum a effacé mes rides en 7 jours"). Sonagi resells, doesn't own — show ritual and texture, not transformations.
- Do NOT cross-post Shorts to YT in 9:16 with TikTok watermark visible. Algorithm penalizes recycled content with competitor branding.
- Do NOT hide that Sonagi is pre-launch. Build the narrative — "the brand is being built in public, you're early."
- Do NOT delete underperforming Shorts. Let them sit; YouTube can resurface a Short months later.
- Do NOT enable mid-roll ads on long-form when monetization unlocks. Beauty audiences hate them. Skip mid-roll, allow only pre-roll, reassess at 50K subs.
- Do NOT optimize for ad revenue. Optimize for sonagibeauty.com traffic and waitlist conversion.
