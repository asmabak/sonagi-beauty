# Sonagi Beauty — Social Media Director Playbook

**Version 1.0 · 18 April 2026 · Read this first.**

This is the operating bible. It's ~4,000 words of the decisive strategy across every platform, tied to a 12-hour weekly rhythm and a 90-day roadmap. Each section links to a deep-dive playbook with the full 41,000-word detail stack.

## 1. The unfair advantage (read this first)

Six parallel research agents scanned the French K-beauty landscape. All six came back with the same finding:

> **There is no dominant French K-beauty creator on any platform — not Instagram (nothing >100k), not TikTok (not a single French-language K-beauty educator), not Pinterest (the lane is empty), not YouTube (Liah Yoo / James Welsh equivalents don't exist in French).**

The French K-beauty space is dominated by **retailers** (Yoonah, Skinorea, Holy Skin, Feel My Skin, My K-Beauty Box, MiiN Cosmetics) — not **educators with taste**. That's the lane Sonagi owns if it executes consistently for 90-180 days.

**This is the rare good news.** The playbook below is not "how to compete in a crowded market." It's "how to build the category's first voice before someone else does."

## 2. Strategic verdict

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What's the North Star metric? | **Waitlist email signups attributed to each platform.** Not followers, not likes, not reach. |
| Pre-launch window? | **~3-6 months** until SIRET + VAT + wholesaler catalog land. |
| What's Sonagi selling today? | Brand authority + a promise ("15% de bienvenue" at launch). No shop. |
| What's the product today? | The AI quiz + the email protocol + the content. |

| Primary channels? | **Instagram + TikTok = 70% of effort.** Pinterest + YouTube Shorts = compounding layer. Newsletter = the asset you own. |
| Biggest risk? | **Platform concentration.** TikTok faces EU DSA charges + French minor-protection bill. If Sonagi over-indexes on one channel, a regulatory bump kills growth overnight. Ceiling: no platform >40% of weekly waitlist signups. |

## 7. Engagement OS — the lever most founders skip

Content without engagement does not grow accounts in 2026. Posting is 50% of the job. The other 50% is showing up in other people's feeds — commenting, DMing, replying.

**Daily routine — 30 min split:**
- **Morning (15 min):** scroll 3 saved lists (K-beauty brands · French beauty editors · 15 target creators). Leave 15 substantive comments (not emoji — 2-line expert templates from [`PLAYBOOK-ENGAGEMENT.md`](./PLAYBOOK-ENGAGEMENT.md) §2). Reply-with-story-sticker on 3.
- **Evening (15 min):** reply to every comment + DM on Sonagi's own posts from the day.

**Weekly sprints:**
- **Monday 45 min** — creator outreach sprint (new DMs + follow-ups from the CRM)
- **Friday 30 min** — UGC sweep (repost customer/mention content)
- **+1h for each post on the day it drops** — the 1-Hour Rule: reply to every comment in the first hour, pin the best comment, heart all, follow back quality commenters

**The 2-week rule:** never cold-DM a creator you haven't commented on meaningfully for 14 straight days. By the time you DM, you're a name they recognize.

## 8. The three growth assets that compound

Beyond feed posts, these three assets outlive any single post and become structural growth engines.

### 8.1 — IG Broadcast Channel: "Le Cercle Sonagi"
Private-feeling community channel (Instagram native feature). Drop behind-the-scenes content, early product access, launch countdowns. Subscribers bypass the algorithm entirely — they get every message. **Goal: 200-500 subscribers by launch day = your guaranteed D-day buyers.**

Add a "Rejoins le cercle" sticker on every story for the next 60 days. See [`PLAYBOOK-ENGAGEMENT.md`](./PLAYBOOK-ENGAGEMENT.md) §11.

### 8.2 — Pinterest SEO foundation
Pinterest is a search engine. Pins indexed in 2026 still drive clicks in 2028. Every pin published now is an asset that accrues compound interest for years. This is the single highest-ROI channel for long-tail French K-beauty intent ("routine peau coréenne", "Beauty of Joseon avis", "double nettoyage étapes"). Slow to move (4-6 months to 100k monthly viewers), but **once it moves it outconverts everything.**

See [`PLAYBOOK-PINTEREST.md`](./PLAYBOOK-PINTEREST.md) — 14 founding boards + keyword targeting list.

### 8.3 — The email list
Every piece of content should funnel to the site. Every page captures emails. The quiz emails the protocol. The newsletter ships weekly. Social platforms are rentals — **the email list is owned.** When a Meta outage or TikTok ban hits, the email list is the only thing that still works.

Target by Day 90: **1,500-2,500 waitlist signups.**

## 10. 90-day path — the shape

See [`90-DAY-LAUNCH-PLAN.md`](./90-DAY-LAUNCH-PLAN.md) for week-by-week. High-level shape:

### Month 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1-4)
Goal: operating system in place. All accounts live. Consistent cadence. Engagement OS running. Analytics tracking. Micro-influencer watch-list built (but no DMs yet — you're warming the soil).

Targets: IG 0 → 500 · TikTok 0 → 300 · Pinterest 0 → 5k monthly viewers · 50 waitlist signups

### Month 2 — Acceleration (Weeks 5-8)
Goal: first creator DMs (after 14-day warm-up). First viral attempt — one post per week engineered to reach beyond the follower base. Pinterest output scales to 15-20 pins/week. YouTube Shorts live. First press pitches (Jung Magazine · Premium Beauty News).

Targets: IG 500 → 1,500 · TikTok 300 → 2,000 · Pinterest 5k → 25k MV · 200 waitlist (cumulative)

### Month 3 — Pre-launch sprint (Weeks 9-12)
Goal: density spike. Public launch date announced. 15% welcome offer amplified. Collabs + seeded PR drops. The hype wave.

Targets: IG 1,500 → 3,000 · TikTok 2,000 → 7,500 · Pinterest 25k → 80k MV · 1,500-2,500 waitlist (cumulative)

### Month 4+ (post-launch)
Add long-form YouTube (one 8-12 min video/month). Shift one pillar from "Provocateur" to "Client testimonials" once there are real clients. Creator gifting program activates with actual product. Pinterest keeps compounding.

## 14. The 10 rules (internalize these)

1. **North Star = email signups.** Not followers. Not likes. Track per-platform.
2. **Provocative Educator voice.** Every post should have a strong opinion or teach something specific. Never polite, never corporate.
3. **Batch produce. Always.** Sundays = design / Saturdays = film. Never write a post at 9am because you "should post today."
4. **30 minutes of engagement every day.** Non-negotiable. This compounds harder than any single post.
5. **Never DM cold without 14 days of warm engagement first.** The inbox of a 20k creator has 300 unread DMs — warm names get opened first.
6. **No single platform > 40% of signups.** When the Sunday review shows a platform creeping past 40%, shift creative energy to the laggards.
7. **Every post has a UTM.** Attribution isn't optional. If you can't tell where a signup came from, you can't optimize.
8. **Repurpose ruthlessly.** One concept → 3-5 platform adaptations. 10-20 min per adaptation, not a new production.
9. **Kill fast, compound slow.** If a format is below the kill threshold for 2 consecutive weeks, move on. If it's in the healthy range, keep shipping the same thing even when you're bored of it.
10. **The list is the asset.** Every social effort funnels to email. When platforms break, the list still works.

## 15. How to use this playbook

**The first time you open it:** read sections 1-4 (the verdict + cadence table). Then open [`THIS-WEEK-CHECKLIST.md`](./THIS-WEEK-CHECKLIST.md) and run Week 1.

**Every Sunday for 60 min:** run the weekly review template in [`PLAYBOOK-ANALYTICS.md`](./PLAYBOOK-ANALYTICS.md) §4. Adjust the next week's plan based on what worked.

**When you get stuck on a platform:** open the specific deep-dive. Each one is 3,000-7,500 words of tactical detail.

**Every month on the 1st:** run the monthly review template. Decide what to scale, kill, test.

**This document itself:** living. Update it as you learn what works for Sonagi specifically. The voice-of-brand insights and the real engagement numbers from your own accounts will eventually override generic benchmarks.
