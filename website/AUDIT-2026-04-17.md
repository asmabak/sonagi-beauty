# SONAGI BEAUTY — FE + CREATIVE DIRECTOR AUDIT
**Date:** 17 Apr 2026
**Auditor scope:** 12 HTML pages, shared inline CSS (~625 lines), shared JS, 2 Netlify functions (Stripe + AI quiz). Plain HTML, no framework.

---

## Verdict
The site is **technically functional but creatively timid and architecturally bloated**. It looks like a Shopify theme that read one Aesop article — clean but generic, with several *avoidable* friction points (no inline reviews on product pages, no meta description, no schema, no trust badges, ~625 KB pages from a base64 image bank duplicated across all 12 files = ~7.5 MB of duplicated payload, broken CSS comment, weird mobile hack stack with 4 nested media queries fighting each other). Voice is *almost* there — the testimonials are the best copy on the site — but every page hero defaults back to corporate-pharma French ("Produits authentiques importés de Corée du Sud" repeated literally everywhere). The "Provocative Educator" positioning is invisible in the UI itself.

**Overall: 54 / 100.** Strong skeleton, weak skin. Two weekends of focused work moves it to 78.

## Critical Issues (FIX TODAY)

1. **Broken CSS comment crashes a media-query rule** — `index.html:462` (and replicated in every other page). `\* Tiny screens: hide quiz btn, keep essentials */` — the leading backslash means the `@media(max-width:360px){.quiz-nav-btn{display:none}}` rule is parsed as garbage. On phones <360 px (iPhone SE 1st gen, many Android budgets) the quiz button is **not** hidden as intended. **Fix:** change `\*` to `/*`.

2. **No `meta description` and no Open Graph anywhere** — `index.html:1-10` and all 12 files. You will lose every Google snippet, every WhatsApp/Discord/iMessage preview, every AI-search citation. **Fix:** add `<meta name="description" content="...">`, `<meta property="og:title">`, `<meta property="og:image">`, `<meta property="og:description">`, `<link rel="canonical">` per page. Critical for AI-SEO.

3. **No JSON-LD schema** — `produit.html` should ship `Product` + `AggregateRating` + `Offer` + `Review` schema; `index.html` should ship `Organization` + `Website` + `SearchAction`. Without it: no rich results, no star snippets in Google. **Fix:** inject one `<script type="application/ld+json">` per template.

4. **The page weight bomb: 600+ KB of base64 images embedded inline in EVERY page (`window.QUIZ_IMGS = { ... }`).** `index.html:629–686` — and the same blob is duplicated in all 12 HTML files. That is ~7.5 MB of duplicated payload. **Fix:** move the QUIZ_IMGS dict into `assets/quiz-imgs.js` and `<script src>` it. One file, browser-cached, 11 fewer copies. Cuts each page from ~625 KB to ~30 KB.

5. **No reviews block on product pages** — `produit.html:951-996`. The page shows a generic "★★★★★ 4.9 · 128 avis" line and that's it. The actual review carousel only lives on the homepage. Product detail pages ship with **zero** social proof at the moment of decision. This is the single biggest CRO loss on the site. **Fix:** copy the rev-card markup from `index.html:1167-1247` and inject a `<section class="reviews-block">` between `</div>` (line 996) and `<div class="related-section">` (line 988). Filter to reviews mentioning the displayed product.

6. **Mobile nav: dark-mode hack uses `!important` to force backgrounds, but `mobile-menu` IS in that selector list with `var(--white)` — meaning iOS users in dark mode get a white menu but cream backgrounds, breaking visual consistency.** `index.html:71-77`. Re-test on iPhone with dark appearance.

7. **The "shop is closed" message is broken French and looks like a placeholder** — `netlify/functions/create-checkout.js:38`: `"La boutique ouvre en septembre 2026. Inscrivez-vous pour etre notifie(e) !"` — missing accents on "être notifié(e)". Per your CLAUDE.md hard rule: ALL French content must have proper accents. **Fix:** `"être notifié·e"` (or `"être notifiée"`).

8. **Cart never actually loads from localStorage between pages** — JS `cartItems = []` resets on every navigation. Add to cart on skincare page → click "Marques" → cart is empty. **Fix:** `localStorage.setItem('cart', JSON.stringify(cartItems))` on every mutation, hydrate on DOMContentLoaded.

9. **Hero CTA says "Découvrir" — the *exact* corporate-pharma language to kill** per your brand voice notes. `index.html:932`. "Découvrir" is the most overused word in French e-commerce. The brand voice is "Provocative Educator" — try `Tester maintenant`, `Voir la formule`, or honest copy like `Voir le sérum`. Same on slide 2 ("Voir les SPF" is fine, "Découvrir" is not).

10. **Product page CTA "Ajouter au panier" is dead** — `produit.html:971` has no `onclick`. Click does nothing. **Fix:** wire to `addToCart()` with the product object, then `toggleCart()`.

## What Sonagi Is Doing RIGHT (don't lose these)

- **The reviews are the best copy on the site.** They sound human, specific, regional ("Sophie M. · Paris"), avoid superlative hype. Voice-on-brand. Keep writing them like that.
- **The Sonagi Advisor (AI quiz)** is a real differentiator. Anthropic Claude powering personalized routine recommendations is *exactly* the kind of "Provocative Educator" tooling that beats Soko Glam's static checklist. This is your moat.
- **Pregnancy-safe filtering in the quiz prompt** (line 1920) — most K-beauty sites don't bother. Smart, defensible, kind. Don't lose this.
- **Korean watermark `소나기` decoration** — subtle, on-brand, beautiful. Just clip it on mobile.
- **The peach + navy + cream palette** is restrained and sophisticated. Don't add a fourth accent.
- **No popup on landing** — refreshing. Keep it that way. (Consider one delayed exit-intent popup with the quiz CTA, but don't add an immediate-load one.)
- **Single-page checkout (`panier.html`)** — fewer steps than Shopify default.
- **Free shipping clearly stated above the fold** ("Livraison offerte dès 50€" announce bar, line 690).
- **Rewards system is structurally clean** — 1pt/€, +50 social, +100 referral, +25 review. Industry-standard ratios, transparent.
- **Brand belt of curated brands (CC, BoJ, Mixsoon, SKIN1004, Glow Recipe…)** does serious credibility work for K-beauty literate visitors.
